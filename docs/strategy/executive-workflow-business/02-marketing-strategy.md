# Marketing Strategy

## Marketing Goal

Create enough trust and clarity that the right buyer arrives at a discovery call already understanding:

- the pain you solve
- the workflow wedge
- the kind of systems you work inside
- the scale and shape of the engagement

The goal is not broad reach. The goal is qualified conversations.

## Core Positioning To Market

> I build AI workflow systems that remove executive reporting and coordination bottlenecks for CIO and ops-led teams.

Every marketing asset should make that easier to understand, not more expansive.

## Funnel Model

### Stage 1: Attention

Buyer sees proof-led content about workflow pain, system friction, or executive coordination overhead.

### Stage 2: Resonance

Buyer recognizes their own pain in the examples and sees that the problem is framed operationally, not abstractly.

### Stage 3: Trust

Buyer sees that you have actually built systems, operated at enterprise scale, and can work across the stack.

### Stage 4: Action

Buyer clicks `Schedule a Discovery Call`.

### Stage 5: Commercial Motion

Discovery call -> paid diagnostic -> sprint -> retainer.

## Audience Acquisition Plan

### Primary

- LinkedIn thought leadership and proof posts
- direct outreach to warm or adjacent buyers
- referrals from prior colleagues, clients, and operators
- website as validation and conversion hub

### Secondary

- guest appearances on operator, data, AI, and RevOps podcasts or newsletters
- case-study circulation in relevant peer communities
- selective partnerships with consultants or system integrators who touch similar buyers

### Deferred

- newsletter as primary engine
- SEO-heavy content program
- paid acquisition
- broad multi-channel social strategy

## Lead Magnets

Create assets that demonstrate judgment, not just information volume.

### Priority Lead Magnets

1. `Executive Workflow Audit`
   - a short framework showing where reporting and coordination waste hides
2. `Weekly Readout Redesign Template`
   - before/after model for leadership reporting
3. `AI Workflow Trust Checklist`
   - source-linking, validation, fallback, and human review patterns
4. `Diagnostic Brief Sample`
   - anonymized example of what a paid workflow diagnostic returns

## Campaign Themes

### Theme 1: The Manual Reporting Tax

- executives do not need more dashboards
- they need fewer assembly steps
- reporting debt is operational debt

### Theme 2: AI Without Rip-And-Replace

- the problem is rarely missing tools
- the problem is broken workflows between tools
- the fastest ROI comes from workflow redesign inside the current stack

### Theme 3: Trustworthy AI In Operations

- buyers do not trust black-box outputs
- source links, validation, and fallback paths are the real differentiators
- trust is the wedge, not novelty

### Theme 4: Operator-Led Systems

- strategy without implementation is too soft
- implementation without operating judgment is too brittle
- the offer combines both

## Demand Generation Priorities

### Priority 1

Clarify the category in the market:

- not an AI agency
- not a chatbot builder
- not general consulting
- yes to executive workflow systems

### Priority 2

Use proof to compress trust:

- prior enterprise work
- systems architecture examples
- workflow examples
- outcome-oriented engagement stories

### Priority 3

Make the next step obvious:

- one CTA
- one offer ladder
- one problem space

## 90-Day Marketing Sequence

### Days 1-30: Positioning Lock

- publish positioning-led LinkedIn posts
- update internal messaging assets
- define lead magnets
- create one strong website narrative for the workflow wedge

Success signal:
- people start describing you back to you in workflow language

### Days 31-60: Proof Expansion

- publish more proof-heavy content
- share operator breakdowns of real workflow failure modes
- circulate diagnostic framing to warm network
- test outbound tied to specific workflow pain

Success signal:
- discovery calls mention a specific recurring reporting or coordination pain

### Days 61-90: Conversion Pressure

- publish stronger offer-led content
- tighten case-study and diagnostic assets
- increase direct outreach to best-fit buyers
- use objections and discovery notes to sharpen message

Success signal:
- discovery calls convert into paid diagnostics

## Channel-Specific Role

| Channel | Role |
|--------|------|
| Website | Trust and conversion |
| LinkedIn | Demand capture and category education |
| GitHub | Proof of technical credibility |
| Email | Later-stage nurture only |
| Referrals | Fastest path to early revenue |

## Marketing KPI Stack

### Leading Indicators

- profile views from target operators
- direct messages from relevant buyers
- post saves and qualified comments
- discovery calls booked

### Commercial Indicators

- diagnostic conversion rate
- sprint close rate
- retainer upsell rate

### Quality Filters

- are inbound conversations actually about workflow pain?
- are buyers in the defined ICP?
- is the offer being understood without explanation drift?

## Messaging Guardrails

- Lead with business friction, not tools.
- Lead with workflow outcomes, not automation buzzwords.
- Show systems fluency without sounding like a generic technical consultant.
- Keep the value proposition narrow even when capabilities are broad.
