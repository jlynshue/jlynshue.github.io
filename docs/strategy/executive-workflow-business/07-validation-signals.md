# Validation Signals

## Validation Objective

Determine whether the market will pay for a narrow, productized `Executive Workflow Sprint` sold under a personal brand.

This document is the decision system for whether to:

- continue
- narrow further
- reposition
- pivot

## Core Hypotheses

### H1

There is buyer demand for workflow-specific AI implementations tied to executive reporting and coordination bottlenecks.

### H2

Personal-brand-led credibility is enough to win initial paid diagnostics and sprints.

### H3

The market responds better to workflow language than to broad AI consulting language.

### H4

The paid diagnostic is a viable bridge between interest and sprint conversion.

## Leading Indicators

### Positioning Signals

- people describe your work back to you using workflow language
- discovery calls are about recurring workflow pain, not general AI curiosity
- LinkedIn engagement comes from relevant operator titles

### Offer Signals

- prospects understand the diagnostic and sprint without heavy explanation
- buyers accept the fixed-scope nature of the offer
- price resistance is lower than scope confusion

### Demand Signals

- inbound or outbound conversations consistently connect to one of the core pains
- discovery calls convert to diagnostics
- diagnostics produce sprint-ready problem scopes

## Weekly Metrics

Track weekly:

- number of relevant LinkedIn interactions
- number of discovery calls booked
- number of discovery calls completed
- number of qualified calls
- number of paid diagnostics sold
- number of sprint proposals sent
- number of sprints sold
- average days from first conversation to diagnostic close
- common objections

- date range

## 30-Day Scorecard

### Continue

- 10+ relevant buyer conversations
- 3+ discovery calls with ICP-fit operators
- 1+ paid diagnostic sold

### Narrow

- conversations exist but language is still too broad
- buyers resonate with one specific workflow pain more than others
- strong interest but unclear conversion into diagnostic

### Pivot

- fewer than 3 relevant buyer conversations
- most interest comes from non-ICP audiences
- nobody understands the offer without long explanation

## 60-Day Scorecard

### Continue

- 20+ relevant buyer conversations
- 5+ qualified discovery calls
- 2+ paid diagnostics sold
- 1+ sprint sold

### Narrow

- diagnostics sell but only in one sub-problem
- one buyer persona converts far better than others
- one industry or workflow shape is clearly outperforming

### Pivot

- diagnostic close rate is effectively zero
- the personal-brand-led trust model is not sufficient
- outreach is generating curiosity but not budget-bearing conversations

## 90-Day Scorecard

### Continue

- 3+ paid diagnostics
- 2+ sprints sold
- at least 1 repeatable case-study pattern
- at least 1 buyer source that looks repeatable

### Double Down

- 2+ sprints share the same workflow shape
- one ICP segment converts materially better than others
- price resistance is low relative to buyer pain

### Pivot

- repeated conversations fail to become paid work
- the business keeps getting pulled into unrelated consulting
- the market only wants broad advisory, not the defined offer

## Qualitative Signals To Watch

Strong signals:

- "This is exactly the reporting problem we have."
- "We already have the tools, but the workflow is broken."
- "Can you help us do this in one narrow area first?"

Weak signals:

- "Interesting idea, keep me posted."
- "Can you also build a full platform?"
- "We just want general AI guidance."

## Decision Thresholds

### Double Down On Current Wedge If

- diagnostics are selling
- the workflow category is well understood
- discovery calls consistently surface the same pains

### Narrow The ICP If

- one role converts better than others
- one system stack is dominant
- one workflow use case keeps appearing

### Reposition The Messaging If

- buyers like the capability but not the wording
- workflow language is resonating but current headline is not
- website or social copy is attracting the wrong audience

### Pivot The Offer If

- diagnostics do not convert despite qualified conversations
- buyers need a smaller, cheaper first step
- one adjacent offer proves much easier to close

## Weekly Review Questions

1. What exact pain produced the strongest commercial response this week?
2. Which job titles engaged and which did not?
3. Did any content or outreach asset create qualified calls?
4. Did the market respond more to pain, proof, or offer framing?
5. What should be repeated, tightened, or dropped next week?

## Validation Standard

This business is validated only when the market pays for the narrow workflow offer, not when the market compliments the idea.
