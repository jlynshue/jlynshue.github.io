# Website Branding Strategy

## Website Role

`jonathanlynshue.com` should function as:

1. a credibility filter
2. a conversion point
3. a compact proof dossier

It should not try to be a full agency website, a broad portfolio site, or a generic personal brand home page.

## Core Website Positioning

### Canonical Line

> I build AI workflow systems that remove executive reporting and coordination bottlenecks for CIO and ops-led teams.

### Supporting Line

I help operationally complex teams fix one recurring reporting, decision, or handoff bottleneck across Microsoft 365, Jira, Confluence, and Slack or Teams in 10 business days.

## Brand Voice

### Should Feel Like

- operator-led
- precise
- calm
- high-trust
- technical without being jargon-heavy
- premium without sounding agency-polished

### Should Not Feel Like

- AI hype
- startup-slogan heavy
- generic consultancy language
- broad service marketplace
- thought-leader theater without proof

## Visual Direction

Keep the current editorial, restrained, premium look as the base.

### Preserve

- serif-forward authority in headings
- clean whitespace
- understated premium palette
- operator-grade tone

### Avoid

- neon AI visuals
- gradient-heavy SaaS styling
- stock AI imagery
- overly playful product marketing patterns

## Homepage Narrative

The homepage should move in this order:

1. What you do
2. Who it is for
3. What problem you solve
4. How the offer works
5. Why you are credible
6. What to do next

## Proof Hierarchy

Show proof in this order:

1. enterprise operating credibility
2. workflow and governance outcomes
3. systems complexity handled
4. recognizable brand history
5. technical depth as support, not lead

Do not lead with edge AI unless the page is intentionally about that service line.

## Section Architecture

### 1. Hero

Purpose:
- instantly explain the business
- qualify the buyer
- point to one CTA

Hero draft:

- Headline:
  - `I build AI workflow systems that remove executive reporting and coordination bottlenecks.`
- Subhead:
  - `For CIO, COO, and ops-led teams using Microsoft 365, Jira, Confluence, and Slack or Teams. One workflow. Ten business days. Clearer decisions. Less reporting drag.`
- Primary CTA:
  - `Schedule a Discovery Call`
- Secondary optional link:
  - `See How The Sprint Works`

### 2. Proof Strip

Purpose:
- establish that the work is grounded in real operating environments

Content:
- recognizable brands
- one-line proof points
- no vanity overload

### 3. Problem Section

Title:
- `Where Executive Workflows Break`

Content:
- reporting stitched together manually
- decisions delayed by scattered evidence
- action items lost between meetings, docs, and chat
- AI outputs not trusted because they are not source-linked

### 4. Offer Section

Title:
- `The Executive Workflow Sprint`

Content:
- 3-day paid diagnostic
- 10-day sprint
- 1 workflow, max 4 systems
- source-linked output and handoff

### 5. Engagement Examples

Purpose:
- prove operating depth
- show systems, governance, and execution credibility

Framing:
- outcomes and complexity handled
- less resume narrative, more problem/outcome narrative

### 6. How It Works

Title:
- `From Workflow Friction To Working System`

Steps:
- discovery call
- paid diagnostic
- 10-day sprint
- optional retainer

### 7. Fit Filter

Title:
- `Best Fit`

Content:
- right for ops-heavy teams with recurring reporting or coordination pain
- not right for generic app builds, vague AI strategy, or low-cost automation requests

### 8. Contact CTA

Title:
- `Schedule a Discovery Call`

Content:
- limited engagements
- narrow focus
- clear next step

## CTA Hierarchy

### Primary CTA

`Schedule a Discovery Call`

### Secondary CTA

`See How The Sprint Works`

### Never Use As Primary

- contact me
- work with me
- let's build something
- book time

The CTA should imply a business conversation, not an open-ended inquiry.

## Copy Principles

1. Lead with buyer pain, not your resume.
2. Use workflow language more than technology language.
3. Keep every section tied to one recurring bottleneck.
4. Prefer specificity over breadth.
5. Use named tools only when they help qualification.
6. Every proof point should support trust, speed, or clarity.

## Services Reframe

The current site services are too broad. Future external copy should collapse them into a workflow-centered frame.

### Replace Broad Categories With

1. `Executive Workflow Sprint`
   - remove one recurring reporting or coordination bottleneck
2. `Workflow Diagnostic`
   - map, quantify, and scope the right fix
3. `Workflow Tuning Retainer`
   - stabilize and extend working systems

### Support Capabilities, Not Front-Door Services

- executive systems design
- cross-system integration
- analytics and governance
- automation architecture
- technical delivery

## Contact Section Copy Direction

Talk less about all the things you have done and more about who should raise their hand now.

Recommended framing:

- you work with a limited number of teams
- best fit is recurring reporting, decision, or handoff pain
- if they already have tools but not a working workflow, this is for them

## Website Success Criteria

The site succeeds when a qualified buyer can understand:

- what you do
- whether they are a fit
- how to buy the first step
- why they should trust you

without needing a call to decode the offer.
