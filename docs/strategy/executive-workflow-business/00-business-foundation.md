# Business Foundation

## Business Summary

### Canonical Positioning

> I build AI workflow systems that remove executive reporting and coordination bottlenecks for CIO and ops-led teams.

### Plain-English Version

This business helps operationally complex teams eliminate one recurring reporting, decision, or handoff bottleneck without forcing a rip-and-replace of their current tools.

### Brand Architecture

- Operating brand: `jonathanlynshue.com`
- Market posture: personal-brand-led operator with a structured offer
- Trust source: senior operator credibility, cross-system delivery experience, and visible proof
- Company posture: lightweight and secondary for now

## Vision

Build the most trusted operator-led practice for executive workflow systems: the layer between scattered tools and decisive action.

## Mission

Help CIO, COO, and ops-led teams make one important recurring workflow faster, cleaner, and more trustworthy in 10 business days.

## Business Thesis

- Most companies do not need a full internal AI platform team to create value.
- They do need one painful recurring workflow fixed now.
- The fastest path to revenue is a narrowly scoped, outcome-led implementation.
- Buyers are more willing to approve a sub-`$15k` workflow sprint than a vague transformation project.
- Personal-brand credibility is an advantage at this stage because the buyer is buying judgment plus execution, not a scaled headcount story.

## Primary ICP

### Core Buyer

U.S.-based CIO, COO, Head of Ops, PMO lead, or equivalent at operationally complex teams using Microsoft 365 plus Jira/Confluence and Slack or Teams.

### Ideal Environment

- 50-2,000 employee organization or similarly complex operating environment
- Multiple systems already in place
- Recurring executive reporting or cross-functional coordination pain
- No appetite for a long transformation program
- Budget authority or sponsor authority for a sub-`$15k` pilot

### Best-Fit Company Patterns

- Services businesses with cross-functional delivery teams
- Operationally complex startups entering scale mode
- Mid-market companies where reporting is still stitched together manually
- Teams with growing AI interest but low trust in current outputs

### Exclusions

- Solo founders looking for cheap automation
- Companies wanting a broad AI strategy deck without implementation
- Teams that need a full custom product build
- Buyers who want speculative SaaS before proven demand
- Buyers without workflow pain tied to real operating costs

## Core Pains

1. Weekly or monthly leadership readouts are manually assembled from too many systems.
2. Important decisions get delayed because the evidence is scattered across docs, tickets, meetings, and chat.
3. Teams do not trust automated outputs because there is no clear source trail or human review step.
4. Status updates consume senior operator time that should be spent on decisions and execution.
5. Existing tools are individually fine, but the workflow between them is broken.

## Market Wedge

The wedge is not "AI transformation."

The wedge is:

- executive readouts
- decision prep
- action routing
- handoff visibility
- cross-system reporting

This is where workflow pain is high, budgets are accessible, and ROI can be demonstrated quickly.

## Offer Ladder

| Stage | Name | Purpose | Price |
|------|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Discovery Call | Qualify the pain, buyer, and workflow | Free |
| 2 | Workflow Diagnostic | Map one workflow, quantify waste, define implementation path | `$2.5k` |
| 3 | Executive Workflow Sprint | Implement one workflow in 10 business days | `$12k` |
| 4 | Workflow Tuning Retainer | Expand, tune, monitor, and harden | `$3.5k/month` |

## Pricing Defaults

- Diagnostic: `3 business days`, `$2.5k`, fully credited if sprint closes within 7 days
- Sprint: `10 business days`, `$12k`, one workflow, max 4 systems, max 2 user roles, 60/40 payment structure
- Retainer: `$3.5k/month`, ongoing tuning, one incremental improvement per month, monitoring and adoption support

These are default starting prices, not permanent truths. Any changes must be reflected across the pack.

## Business Model

- Revenue model: fixed-fee productized services first
- Demand model: LinkedIn, warm outbound, referrals, proof-led website
- Delivery model: founder-led with selective tooling and automation leverage
- Margin model: high-value narrow scope, low coordination overhead, reusable internal templates
- Expansion model: diagnostic -> sprint -> retainer -> referral/case study -> repeat

## Positioning Statement

I help operations-heavy teams remove one recurring executive workflow bottleneck across Microsoft 365, Jira, Confluence, and Slack or Teams in 10 business days.

## Why This Positioning Works

- It is outcome-led, not technology-led.
- It matches your real delivery background: systems, governance, integration, analytics, ops visibility.
- It gives buyers a concrete problem and a concrete time box.
- It creates a bridge from consulting credibility to later productization without pretending the business is already software-first.

## Strategic Advantages

1. You can talk strategy and build the implementation.
2. You already have proof in enterprise data, governance, reporting, and workflow systems.
3. You can translate executive pain into systems design without needing a larger team.
4. The work can be packaged tightly enough to sell quickly and deliver reliably.

## Non-Goals

- Do not compete on low-cost automation.
- Do not sell broad AI advisory retainers without a workflow wedge.
- Do not lead with edge AI, full-stack engineering, or fractional CIO language unless it supports the workflow pitch.
- Do not position as a software startup before repeated paid validation exists.
- Do not take on unrelated builds that weaken category clarity.

## Decision Rules

- If the buyer cannot name a recurring workflow pain, do not sell.
- If the buyer wants custom software instead of a workflow outcome, do not force-fit the sprint.
- If the workflow touches more than 4 systems or requires organization-wide change, split into diagnostic first.
- If demand proves strongest in one vertical, narrow the brand around that vertical only after repeated wins.

## Success Definition

The business is working when it can be described in one sentence, sold in one call, and delivered through one repeatable motion.
