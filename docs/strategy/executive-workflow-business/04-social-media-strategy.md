# Social Media Strategy

## Social Strategy Summary

Social is for demand generation and trust compression, not brand theater.

The social strategy is:

- LinkedIn first
- proof-led
- operator voice
- workflow category creation

## Primary Channel

### LinkedIn

Role:
- educate the market on workflow pain
- demonstrate operator credibility
- create discovery call demand
- stay adjacent to buyer conversations

This is the only channel that matters in the first phase.

## Secondary Channels

### GitHub

Role:
- technical proof
- supporting artifact for buyers who want credibility
- not a demand generation engine

### X / Other Social

Role:
- optional repurposing only
- no primary investment

### Email / Newsletter

Role:
- future nurture asset
- only after content inventory and repeatable publishing exist

## Audience On Social

- CIOs
- COOs
- Heads of Ops
- PMO leaders
- RevOps / BizOps adjacent operators
- data and systems leaders who influence workflow decisions

## Content Objectives

1. Name the problem better than competitors.
2. Show that workflow pain is a systems problem, not a productivity-hack problem.
3. Build trust that you can both diagnose and implement.
4. Generate discovery calls from a narrow audience.

## LinkedIn Cadence

### Weekly Cadence

- 3 posts per week
- 1 operator observation
- 1 framework or teardown
- 1 proof or case-style post

### Monthly Cadence

- 12 standard posts
- 1 longer-form article or essay
- 1 offer-adjacent post explicitly tied to the diagnostic or sprint

## Post Formats

### Format 1: Operator Observation

Use when:
- you want to name a recurring workflow failure

Examples:
- why leadership reporting is still manual in companies with expensive tools
- why AI adoption fails when outputs are not source-linked

### Format 2: Workflow Breakdown

Use when:
- you want to explain how a broken process actually works

Examples:
- the 7 hidden steps inside a weekly exec readout
- where cross-system handoffs fail between docs, tickets, and chat

### Format 3: Proof Post

Use when:
- you want to show real outcomes, scale, or system complexity handled

Examples:
- enterprise analytics governance outcome
- workflow trust pattern you implemented
- cross-functional operating challenge solved

### Format 4: Offer-Adjacent Post

Use when:
- you want to convert attention into calls

Examples:
- signs your leadership workflow needs a redesign
- what a 3-day workflow diagnostic actually returns

## Proof Mechanics

Every week should include at least one of:

- a specific system pattern
- a measurable outcome
- a repeatable framework
- a constraint that signals real operator experience

Avoid soft opinion without evidence.

## Audience-Building Loops

### Loop 1: Insight -> Comment -> DM

- publish a sharp workflow insight
- attract comments from operators
- move relevant conversations to DMs

### Loop 2: Proof -> Profile Visit -> Site Visit

- publish outcome-oriented proof
- drive profile and website interest
- convert through `Schedule a Discovery Call`

### Loop 3: Framework -> Save -> Future Conversion

- publish useful frameworks
- make them reference-worthy
- build familiarity before direct outreach

## Social Rules

### Post More Of

- operating pain
- workflow diagrams in words
- concrete buyer language
- trust and validation patterns
- lessons from real systems work

### Post Less Of

- generic AI optimism
- vague motivational advice
- broad futurism
- tool screenshots without context
- personal updates unrelated to the business wedge

## What Not To Post

- "10 AI tools you need today" style content
- broad technical tutorials not tied to buyer pain
- edge AI content that confuses the workflow offer
- content that sounds like an agency selling any technical service
- lifestyle personal-brand content that weakens authority

## Social Success Metrics

### Leading

- comments from target operators
- inbound messages from relevant buyers
- profile views from job titles in the ICP
- discovery calls from social traffic

### Quality Filters

- are the right buyers engaging?
- are they using your workflow language back to you?
- are posts creating commercial conversations, not just reach?

## Social Standard

If a post does not strengthen your category as the person who fixes executive workflow bottlenecks, it should not be published.
