# Sales Strategy

## Sales Thesis

The fastest path to revenue is not broad inbound.

It is:

- clear positioning
- warm or adjacent outbound
- proof-backed discovery
- paid diagnostic
- tightly scoped sprint

## Primary Sales Motion

Discovery call -> paid diagnostic -> `Executive Workflow Sprint` -> `Workflow Tuning Retainer`

## ICP Targeting

### Priority Roles

- CIO
- COO
- Head of Ops
- PMO lead
- BizOps / RevOps leader with executive reporting ownership

### Priority Signals

- recurring leadership reporting burden
- cross-functional coordination friction
- current stack includes Microsoft 365, Jira/Confluence, Slack or Teams
- clear owner for the broken workflow
- willingness to fund a pilot

## Account Selection

### Tier 1

Warm network and second-degree operator relationships where trust already exists.

### Tier 2

Adjacent buyers in similar operating environments where you have credible proof and language fit.

### Tier 3

Selective cold accounts that show obvious workflow complexity and public signs of scaling strain.

## Qualification Rubric

Every opportunity should be checked against five requirements.

### Must-Haves

1. One recurring workflow pain
2. One accountable owner or sponsor
3. Existing systems are already in place
4. Measurable cost of current friction
5. Budget path for a sub-`$15k` pilot

If 3 or fewer are true, do not push the sprint.

## Outreach Strategy

### Warm Outbound

Use:

- prior colleagues
- former clients
- operator peers
- referrals from trusted contacts

Message shape:

- reference a credible operating problem
- connect it to recurring workflow pain
- suggest a conversation, not a pitch deck

### Cold Or Adjacent Outbound

Use only when:

- the buyer clearly matches the ICP
- the pain can be stated concretely
- you can point to relevant proof

Message shape:

- one pain
- one reason you reached out
- one proof point
- one CTA: `Schedule a Discovery Call`

## Discovery Call Structure

### Goal

Determine whether the workflow is narrow, painful, and budgeted enough to justify a paid diagnostic.

### Call Flow

1. What recurring workflow is painful right now?
2. What systems are involved?
3. How is it handled today?
4. What does it cost in time, trust, or missed action?
5. Who owns the outcome?
6. What would good look like in 30 days?

### Discovery Outcome

At the end of the call, only three outcomes are allowed:

- no fit
- fit for paid diagnostic
- fit later, with a clear reason

## Paid Diagnostic Offer

### Positioning

The diagnostic is not a teaser. It is a paid decision tool.

### What It Includes

- workflow map
- pain and waste diagnosis
- source system boundary map
- trust and risk analysis
- recommended target workflow
- implementation path

### Commercial Framing

- `3 business days`
- `$2.5k`
- credited toward sprint if the sprint closes within 7 days

## Proposal Structure

Every sprint proposal should include:

1. the current workflow problem
2. the future-state workflow outcome
3. systems included
4. outputs delivered
5. scope boundaries
6. timeline
7. pricing
8. success criteria

## Pipeline Stages

| Stage | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Identified | account matches ICP and pain is plausible |
| Contacted | outreach sent |
| Engaged | relevant reply or conversation started |
| Discovery Scheduled | call booked |
| Qualified | workflow pain, owner, and budget path confirmed |
| Diagnostic Proposed | paid diagnostic offered |
| Diagnostic Sold | diagnostic closed |
| Sprint Proposed | sprint proposal sent |
| Sprint Sold | sprint closed |
| Retainer Candidate | sprint successful and expansion path visible |

## Common Objections

### "We already have the tools."

Response:
- That is usually the point. The problem is often the workflow between tools, not the absence of tools.

### "We are still figuring out our AI strategy."

Response:
- The sprint is deliberately narrow so you do not need a full AI strategy before proving value.

### "Can you just give us a roadmap?"

Response:
- The diagnostic can do that, but it is designed to end in a decision-ready workflow spec, not a vague strategy deck.

### "Can this be bigger?"

Response:
- Possibly later. The first step is intentionally one workflow so it can be implemented and measured quickly.

### "We need to think about budget."

Response:
- The diagnostic is the budget-light way to reduce uncertainty before a larger commitment.

## Close Criteria

Do not close the sprint unless:

- the workflow is narrow enough
- the owner is real
- the systems are known
- success criteria are explicit
- the buyer accepts scope boundaries

## Follow-Up Motion

After every qualified discovery:

1. send a concise recap
2. restate the workflow problem in the buyer's own language
3. recommend either no fit or diagnostic
4. include the clear CTA and timeline

After every diagnostic:

1. summarize the current-state cost
2. present the target workflow
3. define success criteria
4. move directly to sprint scope and close

## Sales Success Metrics

- discovery-to-diagnostic conversion rate
- diagnostic-to-sprint conversion rate
- average sales cycle length
- objection frequency by category
- sprint-to-retainer conversion rate

## Sales Standard

If the buyer conversation is drifting away from a recurring workflow bottleneck and toward generic consulting, tighten the scope or walk away.
