# How to Redesign a Cross-System Workflow Without Ripping Out Your Stack

When teams hear "workflow redesign," they assume it means replacing their
tools. New platform. Migration project. Six-month timeline. Budget request.

It doesn't have to work that way.

The most effective workflow fixes I've seen don't touch the tools at all.
They fix the layer between the tools.

## The Real Problem

Most operationally complex teams use Microsoft 365, Jira, Confluence,
and Slack or Teams. These tools work fine individually. The problem is
what happens between them.

A decision gets made in a meeting. The action item goes into Slack.
The tracking lives in Jira. The context is in Confluence. The status
update gets assembled manually in a Word doc every Friday.

Nobody owns the full path. So someone (usually the most diligent
person in the room) stitches it together by hand.

## A Better Approach: Fix the Workflow, Not the Stack

Here's a framework for redesigning one recurring workflow without
ripping out anything:

### Step 1: Pick One Workflow

Not "all of reporting." Not "the whole coordination process."
One specific, recurring workflow that causes real pain.

Good candidates:
- Weekly executive status readout
- Cross-functional handoff routing
- Leadership decision brief generation
- Risk and blocker escalation

### Step 2: Map the Current State

Walk through every step. Where does data come from? Who touches it?
Where does it get stuck? Where does trust break down?

This usually reveals 5–7 hidden steps that nobody formally owns.

### Step 3: Design the Target State

What would this workflow look like if it were source-linked,
automated where possible, and human-reviewed where necessary?

Key design decisions:
- What systems provide the source data?
- What does the output look like?
- Who reviews before delivery?
- What happens when the system isn't confident?

### Step 4: Implement Inside the Current Stack

This is the critical part. You're not buying new tools.
You're building a workflow layer that connects M365, Jira,
Confluence, and Slack/Teams into a coherent flow.

The output is source-linked. Every data point traces back
to a specific ticket, document, or conversation.

### Step 5: Validate and Hand Off

Before the workflow goes live:
- Validate output quality against real data
- Confirm the human review step works
- Document the fallback path
- Hand off with an admin runbook

## Why This Works

This approach works because it respects three realities:

1. **Your team already has the tools.** They don't need more.
2. **The gap is the workflow layer.** Fix that, and the tools work together.
3. **Trust requires source-linking.** If the output isn't traceable, it won't be adopted.

## The Bottom Line

You don't need to replace your stack. You need to connect it.

One workflow at a time.
