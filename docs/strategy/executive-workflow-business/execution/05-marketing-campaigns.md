# Marketing Campaigns Execution Plan

## Strategic Context

From `02-marketing-strategy.md`: The goal is not broad reach. The goal is qualified conversations. Create enough trust and clarity that the right buyer arrives at a discovery call already understanding the pain you solve, the workflow wedge, the kind of systems you work inside, and the scale of the engagement.

**Pattern applied:** `priority-methodology.md` — Weighted scoring to prioritize campaign activities by revenue impact.

## Campaign 1: Warm Outbound (Week 1–4)

### Priority Score: 4.7 / 5.0

This is the fastest path to first revenue. The strategy explicitly calls this out.

### Target List Assembly (Week 1, Day 1)

Build a list of 50 warm contacts:

**Tier 1 — Strongest (20 contacts):**
- Former colleagues you worked closely with
- Former clients or sponsors
- People who have seen your work firsthand

**Tier 2 — Adjacent (20 contacts):**
- Second-degree connections through trusted contacts
- Operators in similar environments who you have not worked with directly
- People who have engaged with your LinkedIn content

**Tier 3 — Selective Cold (10 contacts):**
- Companies showing obvious workflow complexity (multiple systems, scaling teams)
- CIO/COO/Ops leaders at companies in the 50–2000 employee range
- Public signals: job postings for ops roles, blog posts about scaling pain

### Outreach Sequence

#### Tier 1 Message Template (Warm)

```
Subject: Quick question about [company/team]

Hey [name],

I've been building something new — helping ops-heavy teams fix one recurring 
executive workflow bottleneck (like weekly reporting or cross-functional handoffs) 
across their existing tools.

Thinking about the kind of coordination challenges I saw at [shared context], 
I'm curious: does your team have a recurring reporting or decision-prep workflow 
that still takes more manual work than it should?

Not pitching — genuinely trying to understand where this pain is sharpest right now. 

Would love to hear your perspective if you have 15 minutes.

[name]
```

#### Tier 2 Message Template (Adjacent)

```
Subject: Workflow friction in ops teams

Hi [name],

I've been working with operationally complex teams on a specific problem: 
recurring executive workflows (weekly readouts, decision briefs, cross-functional 
handoffs) that are still manually assembled even with modern tools in place.

I noticed [specific observation about their company/role/public signal].

Would you be open to a quick conversation? I'm exploring whether this pain 
is as common as I think it is.

[name]
```

#### Tier 3 Message Template (Cold)

```
Subject: [One pain point in their language]

Hi [name],

[One sentence referencing their specific situation or a public signal.]

I help [role] teams remove one recurring workflow bottleneck — like a weekly 
leadership readout or cross-functional status update — across tools like 
Microsoft 365, Jira, and Slack.

[One proof point: "Recently helped an ops team cut 5 hours/week from their 
executive reporting cycle."]

If recurring reporting or coordination friction is on your radar, I'd welcome 
a 20-minute conversation.

[name]
📅 [scheduling link]
```

### Outreach Cadence

| Week | Tier 1 | Tier 2 | Tier 3 |
|------|--------|--------|--------|
| Week 1 | 10 messages | 5 messages | 0 |
| Week 2 | 5 messages | 10 messages | 3 messages |
| Week 3 | 5 messages + follow-ups | 5 messages + follow-ups | 5 messages |
| Week 4 | Follow-ups only | Follow-ups only | 2 messages + follow-ups |

### Follow-Up Rules

- **Day 3:** If no response, send a brief follow-up: "Just bumping this in case it got buried. Happy to chat if the timing works."
- **Day 7:** If still no response, send value-add: Share a relevant LinkedIn post or lead magnet. "Thought this might resonate given [context]."
- **Day 14:** Final follow-up: "Totally understand if this isn't a priority. If workflow friction comes up later, I'm here."
- **After 3 touches:** Stop. Move to nurture list (occasional content sharing).

## Campaign 3: LinkedIn Content-To-Call Funnel (Week 1+, Ongoing)

### Priority Score: 4.3 / 5.0

This is the slow-burn engine that compounds over time.

### Funnel Architecture

```
LinkedIn post (pain/proof/framework)
    ↓
Profile visit (headline + about = clear offer)
    ↓
Website visit (homepage = credibility + CTA)
    ↓
Schedule a Discovery Call (Cal.com)
    ↓
Discovery call → Diagnostic → Sprint
```

### Conversion Optimization Points

1. **Post → Profile visit:** Hook quality, specificity, operator voice
2. **Profile → Website:** Headline clarity, Featured section, About section CTA
3. **Website → CTA click:** Homepage narrative, proof strip, offer clarity
4. **CTA → Booked call:** Scheduling friction (Cal.com should be zero-friction)
5. **Call → Diagnostic:** Discovery call quality (see 06-sales-enablement.md)

### Quality Filters

From strategy:

- Are buyers in the defined ICP?
- Is the offer being understood without explanation drift?

## Marketing Decision Rules

- If warm outbound produces zero responses after 20 messages, audit the message and the target list.
- If discovery calls are happening but not converting to diagnostics, audit the call structure (see 06-sales-enablement.md).
- If content is getting engagement but not from ICP titles, audit the content topics and language.
- If one campaign theme dramatically outperforms others, double down on it and cut the weakest.
