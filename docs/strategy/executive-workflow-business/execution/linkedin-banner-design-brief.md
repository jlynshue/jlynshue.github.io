# LinkedIn Banner Design Brief — 4 Variants

> **Dimensions:** 1584 × 396 px (4:1 ratio)  
> **Target Audience:** CIOs, COOs, VP Operations at mid-market and enterprise companies  
> **Brand Voice:** Restrained, editorial, operationally credible — no hype  
> **Colors:** Dark navy/charcoal backgrounds, white/off-white text, optional muted accent (slate blue or warm gray)  
> **Typography:** Clean sans-serif (Inter, DM Sans, or similar)  
> **Website ref:** jonathanlynshue.com (dark, minimal, professional)  
> **Export:** PNG, 1584×396px, RGB, no bleed needed  
> **Constraint:** No stock AI imagery, no neon, no gradients, no abstract swooshes

---

## Variant A: Typographic Statement

**Style direction:** Pure typography on a solid dark field. The words do the work.

**Composition:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                │
│  [left-aligned, large weight]                                  │
│  Executive Workflow Systems                                    │
│                                                                │
│  [smaller, regular weight, muted color]                        │
│  Clarity over noise. One workflow at a time.                   │
│                                                                │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Background:** Flat charcoal (#1C1C1E) or very dark navy (#0F172A)
- **Primary text:** White (#FAFAFA), 60–72pt equivalent, medium weight
- **Secondary text:** Muted gray (#94A3B8), 24–28pt, regular weight
- **Alignment:** Left-aligned with generous left margin (~120px from edge)
- **Vertical centering:** Primary text at ~40% from top, secondary at ~65%

**Rationale:** The simplest option. Immediately communicates what you do. No distractions. Signals confidence — you don't need decoration to make a point. Matches the website's editorial restraint.

**Canva instructions:**
1. Background: solid color #0F172A
2. Add text: "Executive Workflow Systems" — Inter Bold, white, size 60
3. Add text: "Clarity over noise. One workflow at a time." — Inter Regular, #94A3B8, size 24
4. Left-align both, position at left 1/3 of canvas

## Variant B: Architectural Grid

**Style direction:** Subtle structural grid pattern evoking systems architecture and precision.

**Composition:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  · │
│  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  · │
│  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  · [TEXT RIGHT-ALIGNED] ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  · │
│  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  · │
│  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  ·  · │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Background:** Dark navy (#0F172A)
- **Grid:** Faint dot grid or thin line grid in #1E293B (barely visible — 15% opacity)
- **Text:** Right-aligned, right third of canvas
  - "Workflow Systems" — White, 48pt, bold
  - "for executive operations" — #CBD5E1, 20pt, regular
- **Accent:** One single horizontal line (1px, #3B82F6 blue) running from left edge to the text block — like a circuit trace

**Rationale:** The grid signals "systems thinking" and precision without being literal or cheesy. The single accent line adds a subtle architectural feel. Right-alignment avoids the profile photo overlap zone (left side gets covered by the circular headshot on mobile).

**Canva instructions:**
1. Background: #0F172A
2. Add a grid pattern element (Canva Elements → Lines & Shapes → Grid) at 10-15% opacity
3. Add thin horizontal line (#3B82F6, 1px) at vertical center, spanning left 60% of canvas
4. Right-align text block: "Workflow Systems" bold white + "for executive operations" muted

## Variant C: Data Flow Abstraction

**Style direction:** Minimal abstract representation of data moving through a system — dots and connecting lines suggesting workflow routing.

**Composition:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                │
│    ○──────○──────○         Executive Workflow Systems           │
│           │      │                                             │
│           ○──────○──────○  Diagnose · Design · Deliver         │
│                                                                │
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Background:** Very dark (#0A0A0A) or deep slate (#0F172A)
- **Nodes:** 4–6 small circles (8–12px), color #475569 (slate gray)
- **Connections:** Thin lines (1–2px) connecting nodes, color #334155
- **One highlighted node:** Slightly larger (14px), color #3B82F6 (blue) — represents the "current workflow" being optimized
- **Text:** Right half of canvas
  - "Executive Workflow Systems" — White, 40pt, medium weight
  - "Diagnose · Design · Deliver" — #64748B, 18pt

**Rationale:** The node-and-line pattern is universally recognized as "system/workflow" without being a specific tool's diagram. The single highlighted node draws the eye and suggests focused work. Avoids clip art energy — it's abstract enough to be timeless.

**Canva instructions:**
1. Background: #0F172A
2. Draw 5–6 small circles in the left 40% (use Canva circles, fill #475569, no border)
3. Connect with thin lines (#334155)
4. Make one circle slightly larger and blue (#3B82F6)
5. Right-align text on the right 50%

## Variant D: Gradient Border with Negative Space

**Style direction:** Mostly empty dark space with a subtle gradient border at the bottom edge — ultra-minimal, signals premium positioning.

**Composition:**
```
┌────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                │
│                                                                │
│           jonathanlynshue.com                                  │
│                                                                │
│▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓│
└────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Background:** Solid #0F172A (dark navy)
- **Bottom border:** 4–6px gradient strip at the very bottom edge — left to right: #1E40AF → #3B82F6 → #06B6D4 (navy blue → blue → teal). Subtle, not garish.
- **Text:** Centered, single line
  - "jonathanlynshue.com" — #94A3B8 (muted), 22pt, regular weight, letter-spacing +2px
- **Nothing else.** Radical negative space.

**Rationale:** The most premium-feeling option. The empty space signals confidence and exclusivity ("I don't need to fill every pixel to prove my value"). The gradient border adds a single touch of color that catches the eye in a feed full of busy banners. The URL is the only text — it directs buyers to the site where the full offer lives.

**Canva instructions:**
1. Background: #0F172A, full canvas
2. Add a rectangle at the very bottom: full width × 5px height, fill with gradient (left to right: #1E40AF → #3B82F6 → #06B6D4)
3. Center text: "jonathanlynshue.com" — Inter Regular, #94A3B8, 22pt, +2 letter spacing
4. Position text slightly above vertical center

## Recommendation

**For this brand, I recommend Variant A or Variant D:**

- **Variant A** if you want the positioning statement front and center (best for people who land on your profile cold)
- **Variant D** if you want the ultra-premium "less is more" feel (best if your content and headline already do the qualifying)

Both match the website's editorial restraint. Both work on mobile (where the left ~30% gets overlapped by the profile photo circle).
