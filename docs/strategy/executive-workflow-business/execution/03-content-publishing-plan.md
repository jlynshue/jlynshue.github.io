# Content Publishing Plan

## Strategic Context

From `05-content-strategy.md`: Build a compact body of work that proves three things: (1) you understand the buyer's operational pain, (2) you can diagnose workflow failure better than generic AI consultants, (3) you can turn that diagnosis into a paid engagement.

**Pattern applied:** `anthropic-harness-design.md` — Content production follows a Plan → Execute → Verify loop. Each piece is planned against a pillar, executed in one session, and verified against quality criteria before publishing.

## Editorial Pillars

### Pillar 1: Workflow Bottlenecks

**Focus:** Manual reporting tax, coordination drag, hidden steps inside recurring executive workflows
**Purpose:** Name the pain clearly enough that the buyer self-qualifies
**Content ratio:** 35% of all content

### Pillar 2: Systems Integration

**Focus:** Why the stack is not the same as the workflow. How M365, Jira, Confluence, and chat create fragmented decision flows
**Purpose:** Show technical and operational fluency
**Content ratio:** 25% of all content

### Pillar 3: Trustworthy AI In Operations

**Focus:** Source-linking, human review, fallback paths, trust over novelty
**Purpose:** Differentiate from shallow automation work
**Content ratio:** 25% of all content

### Pillar 4: Operator Proof

**Focus:** Enterprise operating examples, governance outcomes, systems built, lessons from real delivery
**Purpose:** Justify why you can sell a premium narrow offer
**Content ratio:** 15% of all content

## Content Production Workflow

**Pattern applied:** `plan-comparison-methodology.md` — "Cherry-pick" the highest-performing content for expansion, not everything.

### The Compounding Content Loop

```
Step 1: Start with one strong operating insight
         ↓
Step 2: Turn it into a LinkedIn post
         ↓
Step 3: Track performance (saves, comments, DMs)
         ↓
Step 4: Expand the best-performing post into a short article
         ↓
Step 5: Break the article into 2–3 derivative posts
         ↓
Step 6: Convert the strongest framework into a lead magnet or sales asset
```

This keeps the content system compact and compoundable. You never start from scratch — you always start from a proven insight.

## Flagship Assets (Lead Magnets)

### Asset 1: Executive Workflow Audit

**Purpose:** Top-of-funnel authority asset
**Format:** PDF checklist or interactive framework
**Content:** Framework showing where reporting and coordination waste hides
**Production timeline:** Week 2
**Distribution:** LinkedIn Featured, website download, DM share

### Asset 2: Weekly Readout Redesign Template

**Purpose:** Bridge from content to diagnostic
**Format:** Before/after template with instructions
**Content:** Model for redesigning leadership reporting workflow
**Production timeline:** Week 4
**Distribution:** LinkedIn article, website download

### Asset 3: AI Workflow Trust Checklist

**Purpose:** Thought leadership plus sales enablement
**Format:** Checklist PDF
**Content:** Source-linking, validation, fallback, and human review patterns
**Production timeline:** Week 6
**Distribution:** LinkedIn Featured, website, outbound attachment

### Asset 4: Diagnostic Brief Sample

**Purpose:** Make the paid diagnostic tangible and easier to buy
**Format:** Anonymized sample diagnostic output (PDF)
**Content:** Workflow map, bottleneck analysis, recommendation — redacted from a real or realistic engagement
**Production timeline:** Week 8
**Distribution:** Website, sales conversations, outbound

## Content Review Criteria

Before publishing, ask:

- [ ] Does this speak to a real buyer pain?
- [ ] Does it reinforce the workflow wedge?
- [ ] Does it signal operator credibility?
- [ ] Does it support the discovery call motion?
- [ ] Is it specific enough that a CIO/COO would recognize their own problem?

If not, it is noise. Do not publish.

## Content Batching Strategy

**Weekly content production session (2 hours, same day each week):**

1. Review last week's performance (10 min)
2. Select 3 topics from the calendar (5 min)
3. Draft all 3 posts (60 min)
4. Edit and finalize (30 min)
5. Schedule using Buffer or native LinkedIn scheduling (15 min)

**Biweekly article session (1.5 hours):**

1. Pick the best-performing post from the last 2 weeks
2. Expand into a 500–800 word article
3. Add depth, examples, and a clear CTA
4. Publish on LinkedIn and cross-post to website blog (if created)

## Content Standard

> The content program succeeds when a qualified buyer can read three pieces and understand both the problem category and the first paid step.
