# Validation Experiments Log

## Strategic Context

From `07-validation-signals.md`: This document is the decision system for whether to continue, narrow, reposition, or pivot. Every experiment should test one of the core hypotheses.

**Pattern applied:** `anthropic-harness-design.md` — Plan → Execute → Verify loop. `priority-os.md` — Central tracking, no fragmentation.

## Core Hypotheses Under Test

### H1: Buyer Demand Exists

There is buyer demand for workflow-specific AI implementations tied to executive reporting and coordination bottlenecks.

### H2: Personal Brand Is Sufficient

Personal-brand-led credibility is enough to win initial paid diagnostics and sprints.

### H3: Workflow Language Resonates

The market responds better to workflow language than to broad AI consulting language.

### H4: Diagnostic Bridge Works

The paid diagnostic is a viable bridge between interest and sprint conversion.

## Weekly Experiment Review Questions

From `07-validation-signals.md`:

1. What exact pain produced the strongest commercial response this week?
2. Which job titles engaged and which did not?
3. Did any content or outreach asset create qualified calls?
4. Did the market respond more to pain, proof, or offer framing?
5. What should be repeated, tightened, or dropped next week?

## Qualitative Signal Tracking

### Strong Signals (Record these)

- "This is exactly the reporting problem we have."
- "We already have the tools, but the workflow is broken."
- "Can you help us do this in one narrow area first?"
- Buyer uses "workflow" language unprompted
- Buyer asks about pricing without prompting

### Weak Signals (Record these too)

- "Interesting idea, keep me posted."
- "Can you also build a full platform?"
- "We just want general AI guidance."
- Interest from non-ICP roles (developers, junior staff)
- Engagement from competitors or peers (not buyers)

## Experiment Standard

> An experiment is only valuable if it changes a decision. If the result does not lead to "do more of X" or "stop doing Y," the experiment was not well-designed.
